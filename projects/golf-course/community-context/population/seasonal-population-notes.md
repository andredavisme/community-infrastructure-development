# Seasonal Population Notes — Coastal Maine Communities

> Data year: 2020 (Census baseline). Sources: US Census ACS 2020, SMPDC, municipal comprehensive plans.

## Key Findings

- Maine has the **highest share of vacant homes of any US state** — over 1/5 of all housing units are vacant for at least part of the year; 72% of those are seasonal/recreational (Census 2020).
- York County vacancy rate: **20.7%** (Census 2020).
- Ogunquit's **71.6% vacancy rate** (ACS 2020) reflects its extreme seasonal character — year-round population ~1,363 swells to ~10,000 in summer.
- The presence of a large seasonal population **fuels businesses and employment** during peak months but **does not add students to schools or demand year-round public services** (Kennebunk comp plan).
- Seasonal rentals (Airbnb/VRBO) are increasingly converting formerly year-round housing stock across all communities.

## Implications for Development Analysis

- Infrastructure sizing (water, sewer, roads) must account for **peak seasonal demand**, not just year-round population.
- **Employment** for a development like a golf course aligns well with the seasonal pattern — construction and operations mirror the local seasonal economy.
- **Financial obligations** (taxes, fees) are paid year-round regardless of seasonal operation, which matters for municipal revenue stability analysis.
- Per-capita impact calculations should specify whether based on **year-round** or **peak** population denominator.
