# Phase 1 Analysis Summary — Personal Property, Homestead & BETE (2022–2024)

**Source:** Maine Revenue Services MVR Statistical Summary Reports (2022, 2023, 2024)  
**Coverage:** All 29 York County municipalities

---

## York County Totals

| Metric | 2022 | 2023 | 2024 | 3-Yr Change |
|--------|------|------|------|-------------|
| Total Personal Property | $587.4M | $645.1M | $683.9M | +16.4% |
| Total Homestead Value | $1,074.5M | $1,096.6M | $1,108.1M | +3.1% |
| Total BETE Value | $647.2M | $708.7M | $758.0M | +17.1% |
| Homestead Accounts | 45,120 | 46,297 | 46,653 | +3.4% |
| BETE Accounts | 998 | 1,035 | 1,147 | +14.9% |

## Key Findings

### Personal Property
- **Sanford** leads in absolute personal property ($144.4M in 2024), reflecting its large industrial/commercial base.
- **Hollis** surged from $40.7M (2022) to $74.7M (2024), a 83% increase driven by industrial expansion.
- **Eliot** jumped $7.5M → $23.0M (+207%) over three years — a notable outlier worth monitoring.
- **Old Orchard Beach** grew steadily: $43.1M → $55.8M, reflecting commercial hospitality growth.

### Homestead Exemptions
- Growth slowed: +1,177 accounts added in 2022→2023, only +356 in 2023→2024.
- **Saco** (4,243 accounts), **Sanford** (3,988), and **Biddeford** (3,243) hold the most exemptions.
- Total value grew only 3.1% over the period — modest compared to personal property and BETE.

### BETE Exemptions
- **North Berwick** dominates: $246.2M in 2024, driven by Pratt & Whitney aerospace manufacturing.
- **Hollis** second at $95.8M; **Biddeford** third at $53.5M.
- **Waterboro** saw a dramatic BETE jump: $21.5M (2023) → $45.8M (2024) — merits further investigation.
- Account growth (+14.9%) outpaced value growth (+17.1%), indicating new businesses qualifying for exemption.
