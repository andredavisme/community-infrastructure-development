What Can Our Community Actually Support?

Before saying yes or no to any infrastructure proposal, communities deserve a clear, honest picture of what they have, what they need, and what the real trade-offs are. This site puts that picture in one place.

See Community Capacity → Compare All Proposals

⚠️ The development scenarios on this site are fictitious. All analysis uses real published benchmarks (USGA, GCSAA, EPA, BLS, Census, MRS). No actual project is proposed or pending.

Three Questions Every Proposal Must Answer

📈

Can we handle the demand?

Water, wastewater, electricity, roads — every project places new load on existing systems. Does the community have enough headroom, or is it already stretched?

View capacity data →
⚖️

Is the environment protected?

Coastal Maine’s wetlands, watersheds, and wellheads are not replaceable. Chemical use, runoff, and impervious surface all carry long-term costs that outlast any developer.

See environmental review →
💵

Does it actually pay its way?

Tax revenue projections are often optimistic. Job numbers rarely account for seasonal or part-time reality. The true fiscal test is whether the project’s long-term costs stay below its long-term contributions.

See fiscal criteria →

How to Use This Site

  1. Start with Community Capacity — understand what the community currently handles and where the limits are.
  2. Review any proposal — Golf Course, Edge, Enterprise, or Hyperscale Data Center — to see what each demands and how it stacks up against those limits.
  3. Open the Comparison Page — view all proposals side-by-side on the same metrics.
  4. Apply the Decision Framework — a plain-language checklist for evaluating any infrastructure proposal, not just these.
