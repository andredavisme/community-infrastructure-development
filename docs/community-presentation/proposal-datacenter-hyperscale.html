⚡ Power & Energy Profile

Hyperscale facilities represent a categorically different level of grid impact. At 300 MW IT load and PUE 1.3, total facility draw is ~390 MW — nearly 3.4 million MWh per year. For context, Maine's entire annual electricity consumption is approximately 12 million MWh.

💧 Water Use — Community-Scale Impact

At ~500 million gallons per year, this proposal's water consumption approaches the scale of the entire community's annual supply. This is not a marginal demand — it is a structural reallocation of community water resources.

⚖️ Environmental Considerations

🔴 Water Withdrawal: MAJOR PERMIT

500M gal/yr from any Maine source triggers full DEP Water Withdrawal Permitting with legislative notification under 38 MRSA §469-A. Likely requires Environmental Impact Assessment and public hearing. Aquifer depletion risk in sand/gravel aquifer areas of York County is well-documented.

🔴 Land Use: Large Irreversible Footprint

200-acre campus. Typical build: 100+ acres impervious surface. Multiple large-scale stormwater detention systems required. If any wetlands present on parcel, Section 404 permitting at this scale triggers Army Corps programmatic review. Habitat fragmentation is a documented impact.

⚠️ Construction Traffic & Air Quality

3-year+ construction phase with 1,000+ heavy vehicle trips per day at peak. Local road infrastructure rated for this? Diesel emissions during construction are not trivial at this scale — community air quality monitoring should be a permit condition.

⚡ Noise & Light Pollution — Permanent

Cooling tower fans, HVAC, and backup generators create a permanent acoustic and visual footprint. Northern Virginia communities adjacent to hyperscale campuses report 55–65 dB ambient noise at property lines. Maine's rural character is materially altered.

💵 Fiscal Reality Check

What the Community Gets

  • ~$10,000,000/yr estimated property tax est. — before TIF
  • 250 FTE year-round operations jobs est. (high wage, few local hires typical)
  • ~2,000 construction jobs (3+ years, non-local workforce common)
  • $3B+ one-time construction activity (most spending leaves state)

What the Community Provides

  • Structural commitment of community water supply (~77% of capacity)
  • New 345kV transmission infrastructure (ratepayer-funded)
  • Full wastewater treatment upgrade for thermal blowdown at scale
  • Road reconstruction across multiple corridors (heavy trucks, 3+ yrs)
  • Permanent land commitment — hyperscale campuses are not reversible
  • High risk: BETR eliminates equipment tax; TIF defers property tax 20+ yrs
  • Downstream communities bear water impact with no compensation
🔴 The asymmetry problem: A hyperscale operator with $3B in construction investment will have legal and financial resources that vastly exceed the community's capacity to negotiate, enforce, or exit a bad agreement. Once approved and built, the community has no leverage. Every condition must be locked in before the first shovel.
Tax estimate: York County mil rate applied to $1B assessed value (conservative; equipment often BETR-exempt). Virginia comparison: Loudoun County collected $250M/yr from DCs but spent $150M+ on infrastructure to support them. Full methodology: methodology.md.
