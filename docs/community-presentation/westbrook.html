💧 Water & Wastewater Capacity

Westbrook is served by the Portland Water District (PWD) for water supply and the Greater Portland Metropolitan Sewer District (GPMSD) for wastewater. Both are regional utilities shared with Portland and neighboring communities.

📌 Water Supply

Portland Water District serves Westbrook via the Sebago Lake system — one of the largest and most reliable public water supplies in New England. Rated capacity significantly exceeds current demand across the service area.

🚽 Wastewater

The Greater Portland Metro Sewer District manages combined flows from Westbrook and surrounding communities. Capacity headroom exists for moderate new development; large industrial users require individual capacity review.

⚖️ Environmental & Land Context

Westbrook sits along the Presumpscot River, which runs through the center of the city. This creates both environmental constraints and amenity value for development proposals.

🌿 Presumpscot River Corridor

Maine’s Shoreland Zoning Act applies within 250 ft of the river. Dam removal efforts on the Presumpscot have restored some fish passage — increasing sensitivity of riparian review.

📌 Industrial Legacy Sites

Former S.D. Warren / Sappi paper mill site and related parcels carry brownfield histories. Development on or near these sites may trigger DEP remediation review.

🏗 Zoning & Growth

Westbrook has actively expanded its growth zones, including the Riverwalk district. The city has been receptive to mixed-use and higher-density residential development in recent years.
