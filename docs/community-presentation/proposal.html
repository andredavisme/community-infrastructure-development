💧 Water: Proposal vs. Community Capacity

A standard 18-hole course uses approximately 520 gpd per irrigated acre (GCSAA average). With ~60 irrigated acres and seasonal peak factor, estimated peak demand is 312,000 gpd.

Community Water Headroom
2,300,000 gpd available
Golf Course Peak Demand
312,000 gpd — 13.6% of headroom
⚠️ Peak season overlap risk: Golf course irrigation peaks in July–August, the same window when Kennebunkport’s population multiplies 3–5×. Effective headroom during peak months is substantially lower than the annual average suggests.
Irrigation benchmark: GCSAA Environmental Stewardship Report 2022; USGA Turfgrass and Environmental Research Online (TERO).

⚖️ Environmental Considerations

⚠️ Chemical Use: YES

Standard turf management program applies. GCSAA benchmark: 2.4 lbs active ingredient/acre/yr fungicide; 4.5 lbs N/1,000 sq ft/yr fertilizer. Buffer zones required adjacent to wetlands and coastal water features per Maine DEP.

📌 Shoreland Zoning Exposure

Coastal Maine sites routinely include areas within 250 ft of protected shoreland. Any work in that zone requires Maine DEP Natural Resources Protection Act (NRPA) review. Permit timeline: typically 4–12 months.

💧 Wellhead Protection

York County has designated wellhead protection areas. Pesticide application near these zones is subject to Maine Board of Pesticides Control and USDA NRCS restrictions. Developer must identify all overlapping zones prior to any application program.

🌿 Land Use Breakdown (150 ac)

Turf (managed): ~60 ac • Native/rough: ~55 ac • Hardscape/buildings: ~15 ac • Buffer/wetland: ~20 ac. Native and buffer acreage provides partial mitigation for chemical and stormwater impacts.

💵 Fiscal Reality Check

Does the proposal’s estimated tax contribution justify its demand on community resources?

What the Community Gets

  • ~$180,000/yr estimated property tax est.
  • 35 FTE jobs est. (many seasonal)
  • $12M one-time construction activity
  • Tourism draw (indirect, difficult to quantify)

What the Community Provides

  • 312,000 gpd peak water capacity commitment
  • Road maintenance for traffic access
  • Emergency services (fire, EMS) coverage for the site
  • Permitting, monitoring, and enforcement staff time
  • Long-term environmental monitoring obligations
💡 Key question: At $180,000/yr in tax revenue, the project pays for its direct municipal services only if those services cost less than ~$15,000/month. That’s a narrow margin in a community with seasonal infrastructure strain.
Tax estimate based on York County mil rate applied to ~150-acre assessed value. GCSAA Employment Data Report 2022 for job range. Full methodology: methodology.md.
