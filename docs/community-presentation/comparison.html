📋 What the Comparison Tells Us

💧

Water is the binding constraint

For coastal Maine communities, water supply headroom — not tax revenue — is the practical ceiling on what can be approved. The hyperscale scenario fails this test outright. The enterprise scenario demands careful modeling of peak-season compounding.

💵

Headline revenue ≠ net benefit

The larger the project, the more likely that TIF/BETR agreements will defer or eliminate tax benefits for 10–20 years. Communities should require net present value analysis of all incentive agreements before any vote.

⚖️

Scale changes leverage

A community can negotiate meaningfully with a small or mid-scale operator. Once a $3B facility is approved and under construction, the balance of power shifts permanently. Every condition must be legally binding before the first permit issues.
