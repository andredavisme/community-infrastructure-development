📋 What the Comparison Tells Us

💧

Water is the binding constraint

For coastal Maine communities, water supply headroom — not tax revenue — is the practical ceiling. Hyperscale DC fails outright. Large multi-family and enterprise DC both demand explicit peak-season modeling before approval.

💵

Housing is the most honest fiscal trade-off

Housing generates real tax revenue without TIF/BETR risk — but it also generates real school and services costs. Mixed-income development is the only scenario that addresses a documented community need while producing a defensible long-term fiscal return.

⚖️

Scale and leverage move together

Communities retain the most negotiating power with small-to-mid scale proposals. Once a $3B data center or a 300-unit development is approved and under construction, leverage shifts to the developer. Every condition must be legally binding before the first permit issues.
