⚡ Power & Energy Profile

Edge data centers are the smallest tier — typically collocated near population nodes to reduce latency. At 10 MW, this proposal draws roughly 87,600 MWh/year at continuous operation (PUE 1.5 applied = ~131,400 MWh total facility draw).

💧 Water Use

Air-cooled edge facilities use far less water than evaporative-cooled mid-to-large DCs. Estimated annual consumption is approximately 500,000 gallons/year — roughly equivalent to 2–3 single-family homes continuously.

⚖️ Environmental Considerations

✅ Chemical Use: MINIMAL

No agricultural chemicals. Battery backup systems (UPS) require lead-acid or lithium battery handling under EPA RCRA. Routine refrigerant management (R-410A or similar) subject to EPA Section 608. Small volume — low risk.

✅ Footprint: LOW

5-acre parcel. Typical build: ~30,000 sq ft hardened single-story structure plus parking and generator pad. Impervious surface ~2–3 acres. Stormwater management required under Maine DEP Chapter 500.

📌 Noise & Lighting

Backup diesel generators create periodic noise during test cycles (monthly, ~30 min). Cooling equipment (CRAC units) generates low-frequency hum 24/7. Dark-sky lighting compliance recommended for rural Maine settings.

⚡ E-Waste Lifecycle

Hardware refresh cycles (3–5 yrs) generate significant e-waste. Maine has an E-Waste Statute (38 MRSA §1610) governing disposal. Contract must include certified e-waste recycling requirement.

  • Grid infrastructure upgrades (may require ratepayer cost share)
  • Road reinforcement for construction equipment
  • Emergency services (hazmat-rated for battery/generator)
  • Permitting and environmental monitoring
  • Potential tax stabilization agreements (TIF risk)
