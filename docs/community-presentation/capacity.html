⚖️ Environmental Capacity

Environmental capacity is not a single number — it’s a set of legal protections and ecological thresholds that constrain what can be built, where, and with what inputs.

📌 Shoreland Zoning

Maine’s Shoreland Zoning Act restricts development within 250 feet of coastal wetlands, rivers, and tidal areas. Any project footprint in that buffer requires Maine DEP review.

💧 Wellhead Protection Zones

York County contains designated wellhead protection zones. Pesticide and fertilizer application near these zones is regulated by the Maine Board of Pesticides Control and USDA NRCS guidance.

🌿 Wetland Adjacency

Coastal Maine sites frequently include jurisdictional wetlands. The Natural Resources Protection Act (NRPA) applies to any work that alters or fills wetland. Mitigation is required; avoidance is preferred.

📋 Chemical Setbacks

Maine DEP and DACF rules require buffer zones between turf chemical application and water bodies. Standard setbacks: 25 ft from surface water; extended near public water supplies.
