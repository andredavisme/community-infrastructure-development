💧 Water & Wastewater Capacity

Portland is served directly by the Portland Water District (PWD) via the Sebago Lake watershed and the Greater Portland Metropolitan Sewer District for wastewater.

📌 Sebago Lake System

One of the cleanest and largest public water supplies in New England. PWD serves Portland and 10+ surrounding communities with substantial reserve capacity.

🚽 Combined Sewer Overflows

Portland has aging combined sewer infrastructure in older neighborhoods. The city is under a long-term CSO abatement plan with DEP, which affects new large-volume connections.

⚖️ Environmental & Land Context

Portland’s urban density and coastal location create layered environmental review requirements for large development proposals.

🌊 Coastal & Shoreland Zoning

Extensive shoreland zoning applies along Casco Bay, Back Cove, and the Fore River. Any large-footprint project near the waterfront faces elevated MDEP and Army Corps review.

📌 Urban Heat & Impervious Cover

Portland’s stormwater management rules are increasingly strict. Large paved footprints (e.g., data centers or surface parking) require on-site stormwater mitigation.

🏗 Infill & Transit Opportunity

Portland actively encourages infill, mixed-use, and transit-oriented development in its peninsula and Bayside neighborhoods. Zoning is more permissive for housing and commercial than greenfield sites.
